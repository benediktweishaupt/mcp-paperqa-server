## Framework Overview

This evaluation framework synthesizes best practices from German-speaking countries and European institutions for assessing PhD exposés in interdisciplinary art/media studies programs. It addresses both traditional theoretical approaches and innovative practice-based methodologies, including computational photography, algorithmic studies, media archaeology, and forensic aesthetics.

## I. Structural Requirements and Standards

### Document Parameters

- **Length**: 10-15 pages (excluding bibliography)
- **Language**: German or English (with institutional approval)
- **Format**: 1.5 line spacing, 12pt font, academic formatting
- **Additional Materials**: Portfolio for practice-based proposals (PDF <5MB)

### Essential Components Checklist

1. **Title and Abstract** (1 page)

- Clear, descriptive title (avoid alliteration)
- 300-word abstract with explicit research question
- Project overview and significance

1. **Research Question/Problem Statement** (1-2 pages)

- Clearly articulated central inquiry
- Justification of research necessity
- Original contribution to knowledge

1. **State of the Art/Literature Review** (2-3 pages)

- Positioning within existing scholarship
- Identification of research gap
- Key theoretical frameworks
- Recent and seminal sources

1. **Methodology** (2-3 pages)

- Detailed research approach
- Integration of theory and practice (if applicable)
- Data collection and analysis methods
- Ethical considerations

1. **Timeline and Work Packages** (1 page)

- Phase-based planning (3-4 years)
- Realistic milestones
- Risk management strategies

1. **Bibliography** (2-3 pages)

- German footnote citation style
- Comprehensive source list
- Primary and secondary materials

## II. Quality Assessment Criteria

### A. Research Design Excellence

**1. Scope and Feasibility**
✓ **Excellent**: Clear boundaries, achievable within 3-4 years, appropriate to resources
✗ **Poor**: Overly ambitious, unclear limits, unrealistic timeline

**2. Theoretical Framework**
✓ **Excellent**: Sophisticated integration of multiple perspectives, clear synthesis
✗ **Poor**: Superficial theory listing, lack of integration, undertheorized

**3. Methodological Rigor**
✓ **Excellent**: Appropriate methods clearly justified, innovative approaches explained
✗ **Poor**: Vague methodology, mismatch with research questions

### B. Interdisciplinary Integration

**1. Disciplinary Synthesis**
✓ **Excellent**: Clear articulation of how disciplines interact and enhance research
✗ **Poor**: False interdisciplinarity, parallel work without integration

**2. Methodological Innovation**
✓ **Excellent**: Novel combinations of methods from different fields
✗ **Poor**: Methodological confusion, incompatible approaches

**3. Collaborative Framework**
✓ **Excellent**: Clear plan for cross-disciplinary supervision and collaboration
✗ **Poor**: No consideration of interdisciplinary challenges

### C. Writing and Presentation

**1. Academic Language (Wissenschaftssprache)**
✓ **Excellent**: Formal tone, precise terminology, impersonal constructions  
✗ **Poor**: Casual language, excessive jargon, grammar errors

**2. Argumentation**
✓ **Excellent**: Clear logical flow, well-supported claims, critical engagement
✗ **Poor**: Scattered ideas, unsupported assertions, descriptive only

**3. Structural Coherence**
✓ **Excellent**: All sections integrated, clear progression of ideas
✗ **Poor**: Disconnected sections, repetitive content, missing elements

## III. Specialized Methodology Assessment

### A. Practice-Based Research Proposals

**Evaluation Points:**

1. Clear distinction between practice-based vs. practice-led approach
1. Articulation of how creative practice generates knowledge
1. Documentation and reflexive strategies
1. Integration of artistic and written components
1. Portfolio quality demonstrating technical/conceptual capabilities

**Quality Indicators:**

- Research questions answerable only through creative practice
- Systematic documentation plan
- Critical reflection framework
- Balance between theory and practice

### B. Computational/Algorithmic Studies

**Evaluation Points:**

1. Balance between technical competency and theoretical sophistication
1. Engagement with software studies and algorithmic culture literature
1. Critical analysis of computational systems as cultural objects
1. Innovative methodological approaches (ethnographic, code studies)
1. Clear articulation of technical requirements

**Quality Indicators:**

- Understanding of algorithms as sociotechnical systems
- Integration of computational and humanities methods
- Critical engagement with digital culture theory

### C. Media Archaeology Approaches

**Evaluation Points:**

1. Grounding in key theorists (Parikka, Ernst, Zielinski)
1. Non-teleological historical approach
1. Material engagement with media technologies
1. Variantological perspectives
1. Creative/experimental methods

**Quality Indicators:**

- Focus on forgotten/obsolete media
- Emphasis on technical operations
- Integration with artistic practice
- Critical temporal analysis

### D. Forensic Aesthetics Methods

**Evaluation Points:**

1. Understanding of counter-forensic approaches
1. Integration of spatial/architectural analysis
1. Evidence-gathering methodologies
1. Ethical considerations in investigative work
1. Public/political engagement strategies

**Quality Indicators:**

- Clear investigative framework
- Technical documentation methods
- Critical engagement with state violence
- Aesthetic-political integration

## IV. Common Pitfalls and Red Flags

### Major Issues to Avoid

1. **Scope Problems**

- Topic too broad (“investigating trust”)
- Multiple unrelated research questions
- Unrealistic geographic coverage

1. **Theoretical Weaknesses**

- Insufficient engagement with literature
- Over-reliance on low-quality sources
- Missing key texts in field

1. **Methodological Confusion**

- Methods inappropriate for questions
- Lack of detail about procedures
- No consideration of limitations

1. **Interdisciplinary Failures**

- Superficial combination of fields
- No clear integration strategy
- Underestimating coordination needs

1. **Language/Presentation Issues**

- Inappropriate tone for German academia
- Poor organization and flow
- Formatting violations

## V. Excellence Indicators

### Characteristics of Outstanding Proposals

1. **Intellectual Innovation**

- Original contribution clearly articulated
- Novel theoretical combinations
- Methodological advancement

1. **Feasible Ambition**

- Significant yet achievable scope
- Clear project boundaries
- Realistic timeline with buffers

1. **Sophisticated Integration**

- Seamless interdisciplinary synthesis
- Theory-practice balance (where applicable)
- Multiple perspectives enhancing core inquiry

1. **Professional Presentation**

- Flawless academic German/English
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Engaging yet scholarly tone

1. **Strategic Positioning**

- Strong faculty alignment
- Clear fit with program strengths
- International relevance

## VI. Evaluation Rubric

### Scoring System (1-5 scale)

**5 - Exceptional**

- Groundbreaking research potential
- Flawless execution
- Ready for immediate admission

**4 - Strong**

- Solid proposal with minor improvements needed
- Clear research contribution
- High likelihood of success

**3 - Adequate**

- Meets basic requirements
- Some significant weaknesses
- Requires substantial revision

**2 - Weak**

- Major conceptual or methodological problems
- Unclear research contribution
- Extensive revision needed

**1 - Unacceptable**

- Fundamental misunderstanding of PhD research
- Multiple critical failures
- Complete reconceptualization required

### Category Weights

- Research Design: 30%
- Theoretical Framework: 25%
- Methodology: 20%
- Writing/Presentation: 15%
- Interdisciplinary Integration: 10%

## VII. Implementation Guidelines

### For Evaluators

1. **Initial Assessment**

- Check structural compliance
- Identify proposal type (theoretical/practice-based)
- Note specialized methodologies

1. **Detailed Evaluation**

- Apply relevant criteria systematically
- Consider field-specific standards
- Assess interdisciplinary integration

1. **Feedback Generation**

- Provide specific, constructive comments
- Highlight strengths and weaknesses
- Suggest concrete improvements

### For Applicants

1. **Pre-Submission Checklist**

- All structural elements present
- Word/page limits observed
- Appropriate academic language
- Clear research contribution

1. **Quality Assurance**

- Supervisor review obtained
- Peer feedback incorporated
- Professional proofreading completed
- Portfolio materials polished

1. **Strategic Considerations**

- Faculty alignment verified
- Program fit demonstrated
- Funding possibilities addressed
- International relevance shown

## Conclusion

This comprehensive framework provides systematic criteria for evaluating PhD exposés in interdisciplinary art/media studies, accounting for the unique challenges of German academic contexts, practice-based methodologies, and emerging fields like computational photography and forensic aesthetics. Excellence requires balancing intellectual ambition with practical feasibility, theoretical sophistication with methodological innovation, and disciplinary depth with interdisciplinary integration.
